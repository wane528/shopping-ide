## How to Choose the Right Kit

### By Damage Type

**Small chips (under 1/2")**: Rain-X or Versachem — simple damage doesn't need premium kits

**Medium chips (1/2" to 1")**: Permatex or J-B Weld — best balance of quality and value

**Star breaks**: Blue Star — designed specifically for radiating crack patterns

**Large chips (over 1")**: 3M or Clearshield — need highest quality resin for visibility

**Long cracks (3-6")**: Blue Star or Clearshield — extra resin and precision required

### By Budget

**Under $10**: Versachem ($8.99) — basic but functional

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Which windshield repair kit is easiest to use?**

Rain-X is the simplest, but Permatex offers the best balance of ease and results. Both have clear instructions suitable for beginners.

**Is Permatex or Rain-X better?**

Permatex is better for most repairs—higher quality resin, better applicator, more versatile. Rain-X is better only if you have a tiny chip and want the cheapest option.

**Are expensive kits worth it?**

For small chips, no—budget kits work fine. For larger or complex damage, premium kits (3M, Blue Star) produce noticeably better results.

**Can any kit fix long cracks?**

Kits can attempt cracks up to 6 inches, but success rates drop significantly over 3 inches. For cracks over 6 inches, professional repair is recommended.
